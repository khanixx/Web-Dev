Recipe: Homemade pizza

  • Prep Time 30 mins
  • Cook Time 30 mins
  • Rise Time 90 mins
  • Total Time 2 hrs 30 mins
  • Servings 4 to 6 servings
  • Yield 2 10-inch pizzas

NOTE: The dough for this pizza yields about 2 pounds, enough for two (1-pound) balls of dough.

Ingredients Quantity
warm water 1/2 cups
active dry yeast 2 1/4 teaspoons
bread flour 3/4 cups
extra virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons
kosher salt 2 teaspoons
sugar 1 teaspoon

Step 1: Proof the yeast

Step 2: Make and knead the pizza dough

Step 3: Let the dough rise

Step 4: Preheat the pizza stone

Step 5: Divide the dough into 2 balls

Step 6: Prep the toppings

Step 7: Flatten the dough ball, and stretch out into a round

Step 8: Brush the dough top with olive oil

Step 9: Sprinkle the pizza peel with cornmeal, put flattened dough on top

Step 10: Spread with tomato sauce and sprinkle with toppings

Step 11: Slide pizza into the oven

Step 12: Bake

Recipe: Italian Sausage Spaghetti

  • Prep Time 10 mins
  • Cook Time 35 mins
  • Total Time 45 mins
  • Servings 3 to 4 servings

NOTE:This spaghetti recipe is light on the sauce, which is more typical of how spaghetti is actually served in Italy. If you would like more sauce, you may want to thin it with water or add more canned tomatoes to the sauce. Make the sauce and boil the spaghetti simultaneously. The sauce should be done in the time it takes to heat the pasta water and cook the spaghetti. Feel free to vary the amount of sausage for the sauce. We use anywhere from 1/2 pound to a full pound of Italian sausage, half sweet, half spicy.

Ingredients Quantity
yellow onion 1
cloves garlic 2
Italian sweet sausage 6
extra virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons
can crushed tomatoes 1
spaghetti 450g
Freshly grated Parmesan cheese 1
Kosher salt 1

Step 1: Sauté the onions and garlic

Step 2: Put the pasta water on to boil

Step 3: Brown the sausage

Step 4: Add the tomatoes, onions, and garlic

Step 5: Boil the spaghetti

Recipe: Chorizo Mac and Cheese

  • Prep Time 10 mins
  • Cook Time 50 mins
  • Total Time 60 mins
  • Servings 4 servings

NOTE:We used El Mexicano brand chorizo for this recipe, as it is very soft, almost like a pâté. It integrates with the sauce better than other styles of Mexican chorizo. This recipe makes a small casserole, you can easily double the ingredients for a large casserole.

Ingredients Quantity
elbow macaroni 2 cups
cup milk 1 cup
cornstarch 1 teaspoon
shredded cheddar cheese 1 cup
Mexican chorizo 6 ounces
minced red onion 1/2 cup
flour 2 tablespoons
lime juice 2 teaspoons
breadcrumbs 2 tablespoons
butter 1 tablespoon

Step 1: Mix the cornstarch with the 2 cheeses in a bowl

Step 2: Heat the milk

Step 3: Cook the macaroni pasta

Step 4: Cook the chorizo and onion

Step 5: Drain macaroni, preheat oven

Step 6: Make the chorizo cheese sauce

Step 7: Add the cooked macaroni to the chorizo cheese sauce. Stir in the lime juice

Step 8: Bake
